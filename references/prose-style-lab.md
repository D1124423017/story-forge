# Prose Style Lab

Use this reference when the user asks for prose voice, narrative style, POV texture, sentence rhythm, imagery, density, humor, literary polish, or a style pass after structure works.

## Core Principle

Style is not decoration. Style controls distance, attention, emotion, authority, texture, and reader trust.

Do not polish style before the scene has dramatic function. If the structure is weak, fix structure first.

## POV Distance

Choose distance deliberately:

* close interior: body sensation, partial thoughts, shame, denial, impulse, selective noticing
* medium interior: clear narration with some interior access and scene-level control
* distant narrative: wider social, historical, ironic, mythic, or observational authority
* unstable distance: useful for horror, grief, dissociation, intoxication, obsession, and unreliable narration

Check:

* Does the narrator know only what this POV can know?
* Does the prose leak emotion through perception instead of explaining emotion?
* Does the distance match the scene's pressure?

## Sentence Rhythm

Sentence rhythm should follow pressure:

* danger: shorter decisions, hard verbs, spatial clarity
* grief: repetition, gaps, concrete objects, resisted thought
* intimacy: hesitation, attention, withheld words, sensory selectivity
* awe: longer syntax, layered image, scale, contrast
* dread: delay, misdirection, quiet detail, incomplete perception

Avoid one-speed prose. A page should breathe.

## Imagery System

Strong books repeat image families without becoming obvious.

Build motifs from:

* protagonist wound
* setting materials
* genre atmosphere
* moral conflict
* recurring objects
* body sensations

Examples:

* memory-loss novel: stains, missing labels, blank ledgers, names swallowed mid-sentence
* war story: mud, metal, ration heat, ringing ears, orders folded into pockets
* court intrigue: ink, seals, sleeves, screens, perfume, overheard steps

## Narrative Density

Control how much the prose asks the reader to process.

High density fits:

* revelation
* world texture through action
* literary compression
* psychological contradiction

Low density fits:

* pursuit
* argument
* combat
* direct emotional turns

If the reader skims, reduce density or move information into conflict.

## Cold and Warm Prose

Cold prose:

* precise, restrained, unsentimental
* useful for trauma, bureaucracy, noir, horror, political systems, emotional numbness
* risk: emotional distance without payoff

Warm prose:

* tactile, intimate, relational, sensory
* useful for family drama, romance, recovery, nostalgia, found-family arcs
* risk: sentimentality or over-explanation

Blend intentionally. A cold scene can fracture into warmth when a character's defense breaks.

## Humor and Irony

Humor should reveal character or pressure, not cancel stakes.

Use:

* defensive jokes
* social misreadings
* bureaucratic absurdity
* private narration that contradicts public speech
* tiny practical problems during large-scale danger

Avoid jokes that make the story stop believing in its own consequences.
