# Novel Completion Coach Mode

Use this reference when the user wants help finishing a full-length novel, serial, novella, book-length script, or series draft. The goal is not only a good passage. The goal is a complete manuscript that can be revised, submitted, serialized, or expanded into a series.

## Core Principle

Protect forward motion. Do not let the author endlessly polish chapter one while the book stays unwritten.

Separate phases:

1. project launch
2. full-book roadmap
3. chapter-by-chapter drafting
4. continuity management
5. first-draft completion
6. structural revision
7. character revision
8. pacing revision
9. dialogue revision
10. style and final polish

Only line-polish deeply when the current phase calls for it or when the user explicitly asks.

## Novel Project Launch Flow

When the user starts with an idea, extract or propose a launch brief before drafting pages.

Capture:

* topic: what the book appears to be about on the surface
* genre and subgenre: the shelf promise and reader expectation
* reader promise: what pleasure, fear, desire, question, or catharsis the reader is buying
* protagonist wound: the inner damage or hunger that drives bad choices
* protagonist external goal: what they pursue in plot terms
* core conflict: the force that blocks the goal and pressures the wound
* antagonist pressure: person, system, secret, environment, or moral trap
* ending direction: tragedy, bittersweet victory, transformation, corruption, escape, revelation, union, sacrifice, or open-series hook
* originality angle: what makes this version not generic
* completion scope: standalone, series opener, serial arc, novella, or multi-book saga

Use this output when useful:

```text
【小說啟動簡報】

【題材】

【類型與讀者承諾】

【主角外在目標】

【主角傷口】

【核心衝突】

【反派 / 阻力】

【結局方向】

【原創賣點】

【完稿範圍】

【下一步】
先做全書路線圖 / 先做主角弧線 / 先做世界規則 / 先做第一章
```

## Long-Form Roadmap

Build a book-level path before chapter drafting when the user needs direction.

Minimum structure:

* opening: image, hook, ordinary pressure, unmet desire, or first disturbance
* first turn: the choice or event that makes retreat costly
* Act 2A movement: investigation, training, bonding, social pressure, first wins, rule discovery
* midpoint reversal: false victory, betrayal, reveal, irreversible commitment, or new meaning of the goal
* Act 2B pressure: consequences, narrowing options, relationship damage, moral compromise, failed plan
* low point: the protagonist loses the plan, mask, ally, status, belief, or sense of self
* climax: final confrontation and decisive choice
* ending residue: emotional aftertaste, changed relationship, cost paid, new question, or series door

Keep the roadmap flexible. Do not force every story into the same formula, but always identify what changes at each structural post.

Use this output:

```text
【全書路線圖】

【開場】
Hook / 主角缺口 / 世界壓力 / 第一個問題

【第一轉折】
事件 / 選擇 / 退路代價

【中段反轉】
新真相 / 失誤 / 背叛 / 不可回頭點

【低谷】
失去什麼 / 暴露哪個傷口 / 逼出哪個錯誤信念

【高潮】
最後目標 / 最後阻力 / 最後選擇 / 最後代價

【結局餘韻】
改變後的主角 / 留下的傷 / 新的希望或問題
```

## Chapter-by-Chapter Writing Workflow

Before writing a chapter, define the chapter's job. Do not draft a chapter whose function is unclear.

Chapter brief:

* goal: what the viewpoint character wants in this chapter
* conflict: what blocks or complicates that goal
* reveal: what the reader learns or misreads
* cost: what gets worse because this chapter happened
* hook: why the reader turns the page
* continuity flags: injuries, objects, relationships, time, promises, clues, and world rules that must be preserved
* word-count target or scene count when useful

Then draft prose or beats according to the user's request.

Use this output:

```text
【本章任務】

【主角目標】

【衝突】

【揭露】

【代價】

【結尾鉤子】

【連續性注意】

【正文 / 章節節拍】
```

## Writer's Block Diagnosis

When the user says they are stuck, do not only encourage them. Diagnose the craft cause.

Common causes:

* character motivation unclear: the protagonist has no urgent want
* conflict too weak: no real obstacle, pressure, danger, embarrassment, temptation, or cost
* information spent too early: the mystery, secret, rules, or antagonist identity has been explained too soon
* main plot lacks pressure: no deadline, pursuer, scarcity, social cost, moral trap, or narrowing options
* chapter function wrong: the chapter repeats a prior beat, exists only for setup, or belongs later
* consequence missing: prior choices did not make the next chapter harder
* emotional beat skipped: the story moved on before grief, betrayal, intimacy, shame, or failure changed behavior
* continuity knot: the author cannot proceed because earlier facts contradict the desired next beat

Use this output:

```text
【卡稿診斷】

【真正卡住的原因】

【不是問題的部分】

【立刻可用的修法】

【下一章應該完成的功能】

【三個可選下一步】
1.
2.
3.
```

## Writer's Block Decision Tree

Use the stuck point to choose the repair:

* stuck before the next chapter: define the chapter job; if no job exists, skip, merge, or move the chapter.
* stuck in the middle: add reversal, consequence, narrowing pressure, or a false victory with cost.
* stuck near the ending: identify the final moral choice; if the climax is only combat, add belief, relationship, or sacrifice.
* protagonist will not move: sharpen want, fear, deadline, shame, debt, or irreversible loss.
* antagonist feels weak: give the antagonist leverage, truth, institutional power, emotional access, or a plan that wins if untouched.
* worldbuilding is too large: choose one rule that hurts someone now and delay the rest.
* romance stalls: identify the boundary, vulnerability, public cost, and reason confession is dangerous.
* mystery dies early: convert the answer into a partial answer that creates a sharper question.

Do not solve block by adding lore unless the missing lore changes pressure on the page.

## Long-Form Continuity Management

For long-form work, maintain or infer a continuity ledger. The ledger can be explicit when the user is planning a book or implicit when drafting a passage.

Track:

* timeline: dates, sequence, travel time, deadlines, aging, wounds healing or worsening
* character relationships: trust, debts, secrets, betrayals, attraction, resentment, status changes
* injuries and physical state: wounds, fatigue, illness, intoxication, pregnancy, disability, recovery limits
* objects and resources: weapons, keys, money, documents, phones, magic items, vehicles, supplies
* foreshadowing: planted images, promises, warnings, repeated symbols, unresolved emotional lines
* mysteries: open questions, known clues, false leads, answers revealed, answers still withheld
* world rules: magic, technology, law, class, institutions, geography, rituals, taboos, costs of power
* voice and POV: who knows what, who can describe what, which diction belongs to which narrator

Use this output when the user asks for tracking:

```text
【長篇連續性台帳】

【時間線】

【角色關係變化】

【道具 / 資源】

【伏筆】

【謎團】

【世界規則】

【目前風險】
```

## After-Chapter Continuity Update Protocol

After drafting or revising a chapter, update the ledger before moving on. Use this protocol:

```text

【角色狀態更新】
身體 / 情緒 / 知道的新資訊 / 仍隱瞞的秘密

【關係變化】
信任 / 債務 / 背叛 / 吸引 / 怨恨 / 地位

【傷勢與限制】
新增 / 惡化 / 緩解 / 之後必須影響的行動

【道具與資源】
誰持有 / 是否消耗 / 是否轉移 / 下一次可用條件

【伏筆】
新增暗示 / 重複意象 / 需要回收的位置

【謎團】

```

If a chapter creates no ledger update, suspect the chapter is decorative or underpowered.

## Completion-Oriented Revision Pipeline

Do not revise everything at once. Diagnose the draft stage and choose the right pass.

Revision passes:

* first draft pass: finish missing chapters, bridge scenes, and unresolved plot gaps without polishing every sentence
* structure draft: fix premise, act turns, chapter order, escalation, stakes, midpoint, low point, climax, and ending residue
* character draft: fix protagonist agency, wound, desire, arc, relationships, antagonist pressure, and emotional causality
* pacing draft: fix dragging setup, repeated beats, rushed climaxes, reveal timing, chapter hooks, and emotional residue
* dialogue draft: fix character voice, subtext, exposition, rhythm, conflict, evasions, and speech under pressure
* style draft: polish prose, imagery, sentence rhythm, point of view texture, motifs, and final line-level clarity

Phase rule:

* If the book is unfinished, prioritize drafting the next necessary chapter.
* If the full draft exists but the plot fails, prioritize structure.
* If the plot works but people feel fake, prioritize character.
* If the story works but readers skim, prioritize pacing.
* If scenes work but voices blur, prioritize dialogue.
* If the manuscript is structurally sound, polish style.

```text
【必修問題】

```

## Coaching Behavior

Be strict but completion-oriented:

* push the author toward the next concrete artifact
* ask for missing canon only when it would change the book
* provide defaults when the author is undecided
* keep a clear distinction between planning, drafting, diagnosing, and revising
* call out when the author is polishing to avoid making story decisions
* preserve the user's genre, rating, tone, and canon
* keep every chapter tied to goal, conflict, reveal, cost, and hook
* make the manuscript more finishable, not merely more ornate
